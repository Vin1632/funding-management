# Sprint Retrospective Reports

## Sprint 1 Retrospective Report:

### What Went Well:
- Effective collaboration among team members.
- Successful implementation of the user role and permission management feature.
- Timely completion of application submission and tracking features.
- Overachieved by completing dashboards for the next sprint for User, Manager, and Admin:
  - Admin's dashboard allows promotion and demotion of users.
  - Enables changing of access and permissions.
- Team members found using GitHub collaboratively to be slightly challenging.

### What Could Be Improved:
- Need for better estimation of task efforts.
- Improve communication with the client for clearer requirements.

### Insights:
- Choice of database didn't fit our needs and experience.
- Firebase allowed easy Google sign-in and sign-up, but was more difficult to use compared to SQL.
- Opted to use Firebase for security and SQL for daily usage.
- Initially, team members insisted on using plain HTML and CSS, but found it easier to switch to Node.
- Tasks for the coming sprint assigned based on strengths: Zahra and Nthabi on front end, and Anist and Thabiso on backend, with Amaan bridging the logic by doing both.
- Muhammed, while capable at front and backend, ended up doing the majority of documentation for convenience.
- Trend likely to continue.


### Action Items for Next Sprint:
- Refine task estimation process.
- Schedule regular check-ins with the client for requirement clarifications.

### Sprint Planning Meeting (Minutes of Meeting)
**Date:** 24 April 2024  
**Attendees:** Shakeel Malagas (Client) , Amaan Hanslod (Scrum Master), Thabiso Mahlaola, Muhammed Ebrahim, Anist Mampuru & Nthabiseng Ramalepe.

#### Agenda
1. Introduction
2. Project Overview
3. User Story Mapping
4. Acceptance Criteria
5. Technical Feasibility Analysis
6. Sprint Backlog Creation
7. Next Steps

#### Meeting Minutes
**Introduction:**  
The meeting was called to order at 8pm by Amaan Hanslod. Attendees introduced themselves, and the purpose of the meeting was explained.

**Project Overview:**  
The project, aimed at developing a Funding Requests Management System, was discussed, including its goals, objectives, and scope.

**User Story Mapping:**  
The team collaborated with the client to identify user stories for the Funding Requests Management System. The user stories were mapped out, and the project scope was defined accordingly.

**Acceptance Criteria:**  
Acceptance criteria for each user story were defined to ensure that the features meet the client's requirements effectively.

**Technical Feasibility Analysis:**  
A technical feasibility analysis was conducted to ascertain the practicality of the proposed solution. Any potential issues were identified, and strategies for addressing them were discussed.

**Sprint Backlog Creation:**  
Based on the identified user stories, acceptance criteria, and technical feasibility analysis, the sprint backlog for the first sprint was created.

**Next Steps:**  
Agreement was reached on the next steps, including scheduling a follow-up meeting to review progress on the first sprint and address any arising issues.

#### Action Items
1. The team will create a prototype of the Funding Requests Management System interface for feedback from the client.
2. The team will estimate the time, budget, and resources required for the project.
3. The team will establish a communication plan with the client.
